Vegan Toad in the Hole

Description

The dish evolved centuries ago as pieces of meat cooked with batter as a way to stretch out the meat and make a filling meal. There is a story that Toad in the Hole originated from a town in Northumberland. The local golf course was overrun with toads and a chef in the town devised the dish with sausages as a joke to resemble the toads poking their heads out of the holes on the golf course.

Despite the weird name, it is a great tasting dish.

Ingredients

  • Vegan Sausages
  • Unsweetened Soya Milk
  • Chickpea or Garam Flour
  • Vegetable or Sunflower oil
  • Vegan Worcestershire Sauce
  • Self Raising Flour
  • Red Onions

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 220°C/425°F/gas mark 7. Place the sausages in a large metal tray, add the olive oil and cook the sausages for 15 minutes.
  2. In a large bowl, mix the flours and baking powder.
  3. Make a well in the centre and pour in the wet ingredients. Stir it all together until you have a smooth batter. Let the batter rest whilst the sausages cook.
  4. Remove the tray from the oven and pour over the batter. Add in half the red onion and a few sprigs of rosemary. Quickly place the tray back in the oven on the middle shelf for 30 minutes.
